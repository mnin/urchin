Now that spring's here, you may be spending more time outdoors, but nothing will ruin your outdoor adventure like an outbreak of poison oak all over your legs. Try to avoid that uncomfortable experience by searching for [poison oak] on your desktop or mobile phone. You'll find a description of contact dermatitis, the rash's official name, as well as links to information about causes and treatment. More importantly, be sure to switch to images mode to look at the plants you should avoid and remember, "Leaves of three, let it be!" Happy trails.

Beginning today, you can access a wealth of information about the life of Nelson Mandela at archive.nelsonmandela.org/.

Last year we announced a $1.25 million grant to the +Nelson Mandela Centre of Memory to help preserve and digitize thousands of archival documents, photographs and videos about this extraordinary leader.

Today, the new online multimedia archive is ready for you to explore. You can browse through Mandela’s letters, diaries written during his 27 years of imprisonment, notes he made while leading the negotiations that ended apartheid in South Africa, photos and more.

Read more on our blog: http://goo.gl/xNmjp or visit http://archive.nelsonmandela.org to get started.

As of this week, spell checking in +Google Docs is not just any spell checking. Based on the technology that we use to make suggestions about misspelled words in search, our spell checker grows and adapts with the web, rather than relying on a fixed dictionary. That makes it more useful than traditional spell checkers because it can make contextual suggestions—for example, if you type "Icland is an icland" it will correctly suggest "Iceland" for the first typo and "island" for the second.

The new spell checker is available in English documents and presentations right now, but we plan to bring it to more languages soon. Read more on the Docs blog: http://goo.gl/GJqNp
